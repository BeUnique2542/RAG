EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK - ExampleCorp 2026

1. INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE
This handbook explains how ExampleCorp works, what the company expects from employees, and what employees can expect in return. It is a practical guide, not a contract, and may be updated as the company evolves.

2. EMPLOYMENT BASICS
2.1 Employment Types
- Full-time employees typically work 40 hours per week
- Part-time employees work fewer hours with pro-rated benefits
- Fixed-term employees are hired for defined periods
- Contractors must comply with security and conduct requirements

2.2 Probation Policy
- New employees serve a probation period of 3-6 months
- Manager assesses performance, role fit, behavior, and company values alignment
- Probation outcomes: confirmation, extension, or termination with notice

2.3 Working Hours and Flexibility
- Standard working hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM with 1-hour break
- Flexible work arrangements available where operationally feasible
- Remote or hybrid work available by role and location with manager approval

3. LEAVE AND TIME OFF
3.1 Types of Leave
- Annual leave: 18-24 days per year, prorated for part-year service
- Sick leave: Available per local law; medical certificates may be required
- Parental leave: Maternity, paternity, adoption, parental leave per local legislation
- Bereavement leave: Paid or unpaid following death of immediate family member
- Unpaid leave: Additional unpaid leave granted at management and HR discretion

3.2 Accrual and Carryover
- Annual leave accrues monthly over calendar or fiscal year
- Carryover of unused leave limited and may be capped at 5 days
- Accrued but unused leave at termination handled per local law

3.3 Leave Request Procedure
1. Check balance in HR system
2. Submit request through leave management portal with dates and type
3. Manager reviews considering workload and team coverage
4. Manager approves or denies; system notifies employee
5. HR and payroll use records to manage balances

4. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS
4.1 Pay Schedule and Deductions
- Employees paid monthly or bi-weekly via bank transfer
- Statutory deductions (taxes, social security) and authorized voluntary deductions appear on payslip
- Company aims to resolve payroll errors promptly

4.2 Bonus and Incentive Eligibility
- Some roles eligible for bonuses based on individual, team, and company performance
- Eligibility, targets, and payout rules described in role-specific documents
- Bonus decisions at management discretion within applicable plans and local laws

4.3 Benefits Overview
- Competitive benefits package including health insurance, retirement plans, life insurance, wellness programs
- Eligibility (full-time vs part-time) described in local benefits guides
- Employees responsible for reviewing plan documents and enrolling during open enrollment

5. PERFORMANCE AND PROMOTIONS
5.1 Performance Reviews
- Conducted annually with mid-year check-ins where applicable
- Reviews consider goals, competencies, behaviors, and alignment with company values

5.2 Performance Review Procedure
1. Manager and employee agree on goals at review period start
2. Regular check-ins track progress and adjust goals
3. Both complete self/manager reviews in system at review time
4. HR consolidates outcomes for calibration, rewards, and development planning

5.3 Promotions
- Based on sustained high performance, increased responsibilities, role alignment
- Promotion decisions reviewed by HR and leadership for consistency and fairness

5.4 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Used when performance falls below expectations after feedback
- Manager and HR create PIP with clear expectations, support actions, timelines
- Regular meetings during PIP period to review progress
- Outcomes: successful completion, extension, reassignment, or termination

6. CODE OF CONDUCT AND PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR
6.1 Workplace Behavior
- Treat colleagues, customers, partners with respect, fairness, courtesy
- Bullying, harassment, discrimination, or retaliation strictly prohibited

6.2 Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination
- Workplace free from unlawful harassment and discrimination
- Complaints taken seriously, investigated promptly, addressed appropriately

6.3 Conflict of Interest
- Avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests
- Disclose potential conflicts (external employment, family relationships with suppliers)

6.4 Reporting Channels
- Report misconduct to manager, HR, or ethics/whistleblower channel
- Provide detail while respecting privacy
- HR investigates and communicates outcomes within confidentiality limits

7. SECURITY AND DATA PRIVACY
7.1 Data Classification and Acceptable Use
- Information classified: Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted
- Use company systems for legitimate business; comply with acceptable use standards
- No unauthorized sharing, piracy, or illegal content

7.2 Device and Account Security
- All company devices must use approved security controls (passwords, encryption, antivirus)
- Never share passwords or store secrets in plain text
- Never include sample credentials or secrets in documents or code

7.3 Reporting Security Incidents
1. Immediately report suspected incidents (lost device, phishing, unauthorized access, data leak)
2. Do not investigate deeply if evidence may be destroyed
3. Follow instructions from Security and IT

8. IT AND REMOTE WORK
8.1 Approved Tools and Access
- Only IT-approved tools and cloud services for company data
- Access provisioned based on role and least-privilege principles

8.2 VPN and Remote Access
- Remote connections must go through approved VPN or secure access solution
- Do not bypass security controls using unauthorized tools or personal cloud storage

8.3 BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)
- BYOD allowed where permitted by local policy with mobile device management
- Company enforces security settings on BYOD devices accessing company data

8.4 Backup and Data Retention
- Critical business systems backed up per IT standards
- Follow data retention rules; store records in approved repositories

9. HEALTH, SAFETY AND EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
9.1 Workplace Safety Rules
- Follow local health and safety rules, signage, safety officer instructions
- Report unsafe conditions, accidents, near misses immediately

9.2 Emergency Contacts and Evacuation
1. Follow local instructions (fire alarms, announcements) in emergency
2. Evacuate calmly via nearest safe exit to designated assembly point
3. Do not re-enter until authorized by emergency services
4. Use local emergency numbers posted in each office

10. OFFBOARDING AND EXIT
10.1 Notice Periods
- Defined in employment contracts or local law; may differ by role, seniority, jurisdiction
- Either party may initiate termination per contract terms and legal requirements

10.2 Offboarding Procedure
1. Resigning employee submits written resignation to manager and HR
2. HR confirms final working date, notice obligations, next steps in writing
3. Manager and IT coordinate removal of system access
4. Employee returns all company property (laptop, access cards, documents)
5. HR may schedule exit interview for feedback

10.3 Final Pay and Benefits Continuation
- Final pay includes unpaid salary, eligible bonuses, accrued statutory leave per law
- Benefits continuation (insurance extensions/conversions) depends on local law and plan rules

END OF HANDBOOK
